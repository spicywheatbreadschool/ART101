Exercise 1

Circles are an awesome shape, so I threw some ellipse statements behind some for loops, and messed around with the parameters until I got something cool. Then, I wanted to further experiment with the "netting" or shading effect to overlay onto the ellipses.

Project 1

What makes a brush? Usually, you just make a mark where your cursor is, and then someitmes it has color, sometimes there's a certain edge to it, and an texture you could only achieve with crazy labor. Initially, I put my focus into making simple brushes for lineart, and then ended up with 8 in total, including the 1st "brush".
There are some brushes that make shapes, some brushes that make a blotch where your curses is, and some brushes that I accidentally made in the pursuit of another, that ended up looking really cool. I'm satisfied with the "originality" of my brushes; There's a good variety, some are a bit more restrictive and hard to use, yet from what I've managed to create with my examples, there's a ton of potential and freedom at the same time.
My brushes weren't made with the idea of cooperating with one another. Each one was honestly its own happy accident, and so there isn't a general "theme" I want them to draw. Also, I didn't want to put in TOO many keybinds. I gave the freedom of hue, saturation, size, and brush. That's enough for you greedy mongrels.
